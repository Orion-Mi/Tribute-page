Milton Obote, in full Apollo Milton Obote, politician who was Prime Minister and twice President of Uganda. he led his country to independence in 1962, but his two terms in office (both of which were ended by military coups) were consumed by struggles between Uganda’s northern and southern ethnic groups.

Below is the outline of Dr. Obote's biography

    Early Life and Before Independence

  • 1925 Born in Akokoro, Apac district.
  • 1940 he begun his education at the protestant missionary school in Lira, and later attended Gulu Junior Secondary school, Busoga College in Mwiri.
  • 1948-1949 He attended Makerere University in Kampala. Due to engaging in political activities at Makerere, he was expelled before he could graduate. He was then offered scholarships overseas, but British colonial officials prevented him from accepting those scholarships
  • 1950 He moved to Kenya where he worked various jobs including labourer, clerk, and salesman. While in Kenya, Obote became acquainted with Kenyan nationalist leaders working for Kenyan independence and joined Kenya African Union.
  • 1956 Upon returning to Uganda, he joined the political party Uganda National Congress (UNC).
  • 1957 was elected to the colonial Legislative Council.
  • 1959 the UNC split into two factions, with one faction under the leadership of Obote merging with the Uganda People’s Union to form the Uganda People’s Congress (UPC).
  • 1961 Obote represented the UPC at the Ugandan Constitutional Conference, held at Lancaster House, alongside fellow Ugandan politician A. G. Mehta.

    Independence and Beyond

  • 1962 Apollo Milton Obote became the first Prime Minister after a joined coalition with the Buganda royalist party, Kabaka Yekka. He assumed the post on 25 April 1962, appointed by Sir Walter Coutts, then Governor-General of Uganda. On 9 October 1962, Uganda became independent under his leadership.
  • The alliance between Obote and Kabaka Yekka, however, was short-lived. The conflict escalated between the two groups and resulted in Obote sending troops under Idi Amin to attack Buganda. Kabaka Mutesa II fled to Britain. For his efforts, Amin was appointed chief of the Ugandan army.
  • 1966 In an effort to solidify his rule, Obote introduced a new constitution that abolished all the kingdoms and other remnants of federalism in the country. The new constitution also established an executive presidency, which Obote assumed while continuing to serve as prime minister.
  • 1971 While attending a meeting of the British Commonwealth, he was overthrown by Idi Amin.
  • 1979 Idi Amin was ousted by Tanzanian forces aided by Ugandan exiles.
  • 1980 Uganda was governed by an interim Presidential Commission. At the time of the 1980 elections, the chairman of the commission was a close associate of Obote, Paulo Muwanga. UPC won the elections and Obote was elected head of state and ruled for 5 years.
  • 1985 he was forced out of office, a guerrilla war against his rule under General Titi Okello and Brigadier Bazilio Olara-Okello. After his second removal from power, Obote fled to Kenya and later settled in Zambia but continued to play an active role in the UPC.
  • 2005 He died while undergoing medical treatment in Johannesburg, South Africa on October 10, 2005.
